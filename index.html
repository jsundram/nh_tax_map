NH Property Tax Map

About this map

This map shows the total property tax rate for every municipality in New Hampshire, broken down into four components. Hover over a town to see its full breakdown.

Tax rates are expressed in dollars per $1,000 of assessed value. A rate of $20 means a property assessed at $250,000 would owe $5,000 per year in property taxes. Areas shown in gray are unorganized territories or grant lands with no assessed tax rate.

The four components

  • Municipal — funds town services such as roads, police, and fire. Set by each town's budget and voted on at town meeting.
  • County — funds county government, including the county sheriff, courts, and nursing homes. Apportioned across towns within each county.
  • State Education — the Statewide Education Property Tax (SWEPT), authorized by RSA 76:3. The legislature sets a target amount (currently $363 million) to be raised statewide. A uniform tax rate is applied to each town's equalized valuation, so the effective rate per $1,000 varies by town depending on how recently properties have been reassessed relative to market value.
  • Local Education — covers school district spending beyond what the state education tax and state adequacy aid provide, as authorized by RSA 76:8. This is typically the largest component of the tax bill. It varies widely based on each district's budget, enrollment, and property tax base.

Data sources

Tax rate data is published annually by the NH Department of Revenue Administration. Municipal boundaries are from NH GRANIT at the University of New Hampshire. Water body data is from the U.S. Census Bureau TIGER/Line shapefiles. Source code is available on GitHub.
